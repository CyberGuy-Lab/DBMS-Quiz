Question 59 · ER Diagram
Draw the Entity-Relationship (ER) diagram for a University Registration System based on the following scenario.
Scenario:

A University consists of multiple Departments, each identified by a DeptID and DeptName. Each department offers several Courses (CourseID, CourseName, Credits). An Instructor (InstructorID, Name, Specialization) belongs to exactly one department and may teach many courses. A Student (StudentID, Name, DateOfBirth, Email) can enroll in many courses, and each enrollment records a Grade. Each course must be taught by at least one instructor.

Required: Show entities, attributes (with primary keys underlined), relationships with cardinalities (crow's foot notation), and participation constraints.
📐 Model Answer — ER Diagram
Key points to note:
• DEPARTMENT — Strong entity; primary key = DeptID
• COURSE — Strong entity; primary key = CourseID
• INSTRUCTOR — Strong entity; primary key = InstructorID
• STUDENT — Strong entity; primary key = StudentID
• OFFERS: 1 Dept → Many Courses (total participation from Course side)
• BELONGS_TO: Many Instructors → 1 Dept (total from Instructor side)
• TEACHES: Many Instructors ↔ Many Courses (M:N)
• ENROLLED_IN: Many Students ↔ Many Courses (M:N) with Grade as a relationship attribute
• In the relational mapping, ENROLLED_IN and TEACHES become junction tables
Question 60 · Relational Algebra
Given the following relational schema, write Relational Algebra expressions for the queries below.
Schema:
Student(StudentID, Name, Age, DeptID)
Department(DeptID, DeptName)
Course(CourseID, CourseName, Credits, DeptID)
Enrolment(StudentID, CourseID, Grade)

Queries:
a) Find the names of all students enrolled in the department named 'Computer Science'.
b) List the names and ages of students who received a grade of 'A' in any course.
c) Find the names of all courses with more than 3 credits.
d) List the names of students who are enrolled in every course offered by the 'Computer Science' department.
e) Find the names of instructors (from Student relation used as proxy here) who have NOT enrolled in any course. (Use Set-Difference.)
📐 Model Answers — Relational Algebra
a) Names of students in the 'Computer Science' department:
πName( Student ⋈ σDeptName = 'Computer Science'(Department) )
First apply Selection (σ) on Department to get only the CS department record, then Natural Join (⋈) with Student on DeptID, then Project (π) only the Name column.
b) Names and ages of students who received an 'A' grade:
πName, Age( Student ⋈ σGrade = 'A'(Enrolment) )
Selection filters Enrolment rows with Grade='A'. Natural join with Student (on StudentID) brings in Name and Age. Projection removes other columns.
c) Course names with more than 3 credits:
πCourseName( σCredits > 3(Course) )
Apply Selection directly on the Course relation with the predicate Credits > 3, then project only CourseName. This is a single-relation query.
d) Students enrolled in EVERY CS course (Division operation):
CS_Courses ← πCourseID( Course ⋈ σDeptName='Computer Science'(Department) )
Enrolled_IDs ← πStudentID, CourseID(Enrolment)
Result ← πName( Student ⋈ (Enrolled_IDs ÷ CS_Courses) )
Division (÷) answers "for all" queries. CS_Courses gives all CS CourseIDs. Division finds StudentIDs enrolled in ALL of those courses. Join with Student retrieves the names.
e) Students who have NOT enrolled in any course (Set-Difference):
All_Students ← πStudentID(Student)
Enrolled_Students ← πStudentID(Enrolment)
Not_Enrolled ← All_Students − Enrolled_Students
Result ← πName( Student ⋈ Not_Enrolled )
Set-Difference (−) returns StudentIDs in Student that have no corresponding entry in Enrolment. Both operands must be union-compatible (same schema: just StudentID). Join retrieves names.
Key Operators Summary:
σ (Selection) — filters rows  |  π (Projection) — selects columns  |  ⋈ (Natural Join) — joins on common attrs
÷ (Division) — "for all" queries  |  − (Set-Difference) — in R but not S  |  ∪ (Union) — combines both
